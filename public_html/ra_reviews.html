My favorite of his group

As I may have said in earlier reviews, I am new to Silat and with that, this training is suited for those just getting started.  He [Bob Orlando] goes over the basics in great detail and uses analogies to make key points clear.  If you're interested in learning Silat, these DVD's will reinforce the key principles of Silat and make some of the mysitical aspects more clear.  I would highly suggest this set for the Silat newcomer and almost would feel you'd be missing something if you didn't check it out.  For those on the fence, understand that Silat is an excellent fighting system.  If you're the type that wants to do flipping fly kicks and be the next Jet Li, Silat won't be your cup of tea.  If you are however looking to learn a fighting skill that can be applied in an urban situation, look no furthur.  I'd also suggest looking into Kali, also known as Escrima and Arnis as well.  Krav Maga may also be of interest as well as I believe the novice student should be open to the fact that all the Martial Arts offer something.

In close, BUY THIS DVD!!!  You won't be sorry.  Also check out YouTube as I've seen clips of this DVD out there so you can see for yourself.

From Belgium:  Wim Demeere
January 16, 2003

When I first saw the video I had a hard time processing the information they hold.  Not that it wasn't presented in a clear fashion, it is.  It's just that there's so much of it.  Throughout the video, Bob gives you tons of information to work with and many different ways to do the drills.  But it goes further than that.  He not only shows you the drills he uses but goes into detail as to how and why they work.  This gives you the tools you need to devise your own drills, using techniques from your own art.  This will allow you to speed up the learning process tremendously and help ingrain what it teaches.

Throughout the video, he keeps things fun to watch by cracking jokes or whacking his partners and the looks on their faces are priceless.  Especially when Bob works with "Tiny", this stands out.  It is important because you will need to view the video many times over to get the full benefit.  There are the drills themselves but that is just the most obvious part.  Once you start experimenting with them, you will quickly feel the need to watch them again to see how exactly you perform the details of Mr. Orlando's techniques and really make the drills come to life.  Seeing him move is an education in and of itself.  He has extremely good technical skills and the refinement of decades of training is obvious.  Without a doubt, one of the most impressive martial artists I have ever seen.

If you are a Silat, Kuntao or Kali player, this video should be on top of your list of  "Must buy!"  items.  If you train in an art that doesn't emphasize drills, you can use them to create your own.  At any rate, buy the video.  You won't regret it [emphasis added].
